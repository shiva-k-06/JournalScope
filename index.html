How to interpret the results

This tool lists academic journals that have been removed (delisted) from at least one of two major reference databases: Web of Science (WoS) and Scopus. A journal may appear delisted from one or both simultaneously.

Each record includes the year of delisting and, where available, the official reason communicated by the database.

🔴

Red flag — Scopus or WoS

Removal due to questionable editorial practices or failure to meet indexing criteria.

📅

Year of delisting

The year the journal was officially removed from the corresponding index.

⚠️

Reason for delisting

Official reason as communicated by the database. Not always available for every record.

🔁

Multiple databases

A journal may be delisted from both Scopus and WoS with different years and reasons.

Data sources

Data sourced from the official lists published by each indexing database:

Clarivate / Web of Science (WoS)

Official list of journals removed from Web of Science. Clarivate carries out periodic quality reviews that may lead to delistings. Available on the Clarivate support portal. Coverage: from 2023.

Elsevier / Scopus

List of journals removed from Scopus, managed by the Scopus Content Selection & Advisory Board (CSAB). Available on the official Scopus website. Coverage: from 2009.

About this tool

This tool is an independent reference resource to support selection of publication venues. It covers journals delisted from Web of Science and Scopus only.

Coverage: 1,412 delisted journals — 1,126 from Scopus, 383 from WoS, with 97 journals appearing in both databases.

This resource is provided for informational purposes only and has no official status. For formal evaluation processes, please consult the databases and relevant evaluation bodies directly. Verification against original sources is recommended.
